## 6. Important Notes

### Money
- UK uses **British Pounds (GBP)**. ~$1 USD = £0.79
- Contactless cards accepted almost everywhere
- Bring a **no foreign transaction fee** credit card (Chase Sapphire, Capital One Venture, etc.)
- Carry GBP 50-100 cash for small vendors in rural Cornwall

### Getting Around London
- Use **contactless payment** (credit/debit card or phone) on Tube, buses, and trains — daily fare caps apply automatically, same as Oyster
- National Theatre is on the South Bank — walk from Waterloo station
- Selhurst Park (Crystal Palace) is in SE London — take the train from London Bridge or Victoria to Selhurst or Norwood Junction (~25 min)

### Power & Connectivity
- UK uses **Type G plugs** (230V) — bring adapters
- Consider an **eSIM** (Airalo, Three UK) for mobile data
- Free WiFi in most hotels, cafes, and on trains

### Cornwall Transport
- Poldark locations are spread out — a guided tour or rental car is recommended
- UK drives on the **left side**
- Coastal paths can be muddy — sturdy shoes, not sandals

### Time Zone
- UK is on **BST (UTC+1)** in late May = **8 hours ahead** of Pacific Daylight Time

### Theatre Tips
- National Theatre dress code: smart-casual (no flip-flops/beachwear)
- Arrive 30 minutes before curtain
- The NT has riverside bars and a terrace — great for pre-show drinks

### Football Match Tips
- Selhurst Park is an old-school English ground — incredible atmosphere
- All 10 PL final-day matches kick off at 5pm simultaneously — electric energy
- No bags larger than A4 allowed inside; leave backpacks at the hotel
- Pubs around Selhurst fill up early — arrive by 3pm to soak in the atmosphere

### Weather Backup Plans
- **Cornwall rain days:** Eden Project, Tate St Ives, Minack Theatre
- **London rain days:** Free museums (British Museum, V&A, Tate Modern), Borough Market, afternoon tea
