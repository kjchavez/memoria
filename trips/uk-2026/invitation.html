Day by Day

Day 1 — Thu, May 21

Arrival in London

Fly in from San Francisco. Check into our South Bank hotel, then stroll along the Thames past the London Eye, Tate Modern, and the South Bank Book Market. Dinner by the river. Early night — tomorrow is show night!

✈️
Day 2 — Fri, May 22

Les Liaisons Dangereuses ✓

Borough Market for brunch, then St Paul's and the Globe Theatre. Freshen up for the evening. Pre-theatre dinner on the South Bank, then curtain up at 7 PM at the National Theatre. Aidan Turner and Lesley Manville live on stage. Post-show drinks on the Thames terrace.

🎭
Day 3 — Sat, May 23

Classic London Sightseeing

Big Ben, Buckingham Palace, Changing of the Guard. Afternoon in Greenwich — the Old Royal Naval College and its Painted Hall, then Greenwich Park for Thames views. Or the V&A's 18th-century costume galleries for Poldark fans.

🏛️
Day 4 — Sun, May 24

Match Day: Crystal Palace vs Arsenal

Lazy morning, then last London exploring — Camden Market, Covent Garden, or the British Museum. By 3 PM, head to Selhurst Park for pre-match pub atmosphere. 5 PM kickoff — Premier League final day magic. All 10 matches at once.

⚽
Day 5 — Mon, May 25 (Bank Holiday)

Train to Cornwall

Board the GWR from Paddington to Penzance — a 5-hour journey through gorgeous countryside. The train hugs the sea wall at Dawlish (sit on the left!). Arrive, settle in, explore Penzance Promenade with views of St Michael's Mount.

🚂
Day 6 — Tue, May 26

Poldark Day: Botallack & the Tin Coast

The dramatic clifftop engine houses of Botallack Mine ("Wheal Leisure"), Levant Mine's working beam engine, and the South West Coast Path. Optional: St Michael's Mount. Fish and chips on the seafront to end the day.

⛏️
Day 7 — Wed, May 27

Holywell Bay & the Coast

Drive to Holywell Bay — where Ross gallops his horse in the Poldark credits. Sweeping beach, dunes, and sea caves. Lunch in Padstow (Cornish pasties!), then St Ives or the Minack Theatre for a final afternoon.

🏖️
Day 8 — Thu, May 28

Departure

Leisurely Penzance breakfast. Train back to London Paddington, connecting to Heathrow for the flight home. Or fly from Newquay if available. Farewell, England.
